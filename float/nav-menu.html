Nav Menu

A basic nav menu with left-aligned and right-aligned items. This is not a "sticky" menu. It just sits at the top of the document.

HTML Setup

Begin by having two sections in your HTML, a <nav> element, and a <main> element. You'll need both of these, because you need to turn off padding on your page's <body>. Otherwise, your menu will have spacing around it, which you don't want. In this example, I re-apply the spacing to the <main> element. Here is the basic structure: 

<html>
    <body>
        <nav>
        <main>
            <-- menu items go here -->
        </nav>
        <main>
          <-- page content goes here -->
        </main>
    </body>
</html>

That's it. Everything else is done in CSS. You can view source, and read my comments for details.

This example also demonstrates applying the overflow: auto; property to address the clearfix problem, where if all the child block-elements are floating, the parent block element will collapse vertically.
